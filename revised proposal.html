Teacher Experience

Primary users — the ones paying $300/year
Web Portal (Primary Dashboard): Manage classrooms, invite parents, upload school branding (crest, colors, vision statement), assign orientation modules, track parent progress, view analytics, and communicate directly with parents.

Mobile App (On-The-Go Access): Respond to parent messages, view completion reports, send push reminders, and receive real-time notifications — from anywhere.
👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Parent Experience

Secondary users — invited by their child's teacher
Mobile App (Primary Experience): Complete orientation modules, take quizzes, check off tasks, earn tokens and badges, receive push notifications, and communicate directly with their child's teacher.

Web Portal (Full Desktop Access): Everything available in the mobile app, optimized for parents who prefer desktop. Both sync in real time — progress carries across devices.

Why Two Separate Apps?

Teachers and parents have fundamentally different workflows. Combining them into one app creates confusion — cluttered navigation, mismatched features, and a compromised experience for both.

Separate apps mean each user type gets a purpose-built interface designed around exactly what they need. From a technical standpoint, this architecture also makes the platform easier to scale and maintain — we can update teacher features without touching the parent experience, roll out A/B tests per user type, and eventually expand to additional user roles (district administrators, school counselors) without rebuilding the core.

Two-Way Communication — The Core

This was a key priority from our conversation. The messaging system isn't an add-on — it's foundational. Teachers and parents need a direct, secure, always-available channel inside the platform.

The system includes real-time messaging (text, images, attachments), read receipts, push notifications for new messages, message history searchable by date and parent, and broadcast messaging for class-wide announcements. All within the app — no external tools needed.

Admin Dashboard

Web application only — built exclusively for your team at Unparalleled ESS. Full control over teacher accounts, subscription billing (Stripe-powered), orientation content management, platform-wide analytics, and support ticketing. This is your command center for the entire operation.

08 · Orientation & Ongoing Engagement

Onboarding Parents Then Keeping Them

The structured orientation is the onramp — it's what gets parents invested in the platform. Once they complete it, the app becomes their ongoing daily hub for communication, school updates, and teacher collaboration all year long.

📖

Module · Quiz · 3 Tokens

📋

Week 2 — Classroom Expectations

Module · Quiz · Checklist · 5 Tokens
Behavior standards, homework policies, attendance expectations. Parents confirm they've set up the basics at home.
🤝

Week 3 — The Parent Role

Module · Quiz · 5 Tokens
What effective parent support looks like — daily actions to reinforce classroom learning.
💬

Week 4 — Communication Protocols

Module · Quiz · Checklist · 5 Tokens
How and when to reach out to teachers. Urgent vs. scheduled. Parents commit to response timelines.
📊

Week 5 — Tracking Student Progress

Module · Quiz · 5 Tokens

🎓

Week 6 — Graduation & Transition

Final Assessment · Certificate · 10 Tokens

What Happens After Orientation

Orientation graduates parents into the full platform experience. After completing it, parents continue using the app daily for direct messaging with teachers, school announcements, progress updates, new resource modules throughout the year, ongoing token opportunities, and community engagement.

Think of orientation as the onramp. The app is the highway — and parents stay on it all year long.
